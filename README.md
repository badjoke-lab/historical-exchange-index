# Historical Exchange Index

Historical Exchange Index (HEI) is a historical registry of crypto exchanges.

HEI is built to track exchanges as historical records rather than as live rankings.
Its focus is on exchange identity, operating status, major lifecycle events, archived references, and supporting records.

## What HEI does

HEI organizes exchange records around three core layers:

- **Entity**
  - the exchange itself
  - name, aliases, type, status, origin, dates, and summary
- **Event**
  - meaningful changes in the exchange lifecycle
  - launches, shutdowns, suspensions, hacks, acquisitions, rebrands, and other major developments
- **Evidence**
  - supporting records used to back claims
  - archival links, official references, reporting, and other supporting materials

The goal is to make exchange history easier to browse, compare, and verify in a structured archive-style format.

## What HEI is not

HEI is not:

- a trading platform
- a token price tracker
- a live exchange leaderboard
- an exchange recommendation site
- an investment advice product

It is a registry-first project focused on historical structure and record clarity.

## Main features

### Structured exchange records

Each entry is intended to work as a compact dossier rather than a short listing.
HEI is designed to preserve exchange identity over time, including name changes, status shifts, and end-state classification.

### Dead-side and active-side views

HEI separates dead-side and active-side browsing so that the project can serve both historical lookup and current registry browsing without collapsing everything into one flat list.

### Timeline-oriented detail pages

Exchange detail pages are designed to show important lifecycle events in chronological form.
This makes it easier to understand not just what an exchange is, but what happened to it.

### Archive-aware URL handling

HEI treats URL history carefully.
For historical or dead-side records, archived references may be preferred over live-domain assumptions when appropriate.

### Dense registry browsing

HEI is intentionally built as a dense, quiet registry rather than a marketing-heavy product.
The emphasis is on scan speed, clarity, and structured browsing.

## Data quality note

HEI records may be incomplete, approximate, contested, or revised.

Some dates, causes, classifications, and supporting details may change as stronger records become available.
Unknown values are sometimes kept as unknown rather than being forced into a weak conclusion.

## Project direction

HEI aims to feel like a modern archival registry for crypto exchange history:

- structured
- calm
- data-dense
- readable
- evidence-aware
- history-first

The project favors durable records over hype, noise, or growth-style presentation.

## Status

HEI is currently in its early public build stage.

The initial goal is a clean public registry with canonical exchange records, detail pages, methodology pages, and support paths in place before broader expansion.
